Beyond Work

A fuller picture.

— reader, writer, photographer, advocate

Outside of my work at Verizon, I'm a voracious reader, an active writer, and a dedicated advocate for the causes I care about. I publish data-driven analyses on the U.S. economy and geopolitical shifts on my Substack, and I volunteer as an official event photographer for Orlando Pride.

A lifelong outdoorsman, an Eagle Scout, and a Vigil Honor recipient in the Order of the Arrow, I'm happiest when I'm engineering a predictive model, dissecting global economics and foreign affairs, or out on the water kayaking. I bring a rigorous, data-driven approach to everything I do — grounded in a deeply-held belief that the best work leaves systems fundamentally better than we found them.

03

Community & Advocacy

Orlando Pride

I volunteer as an official event photographer for Orlando Pride, and I offer free photographic services to the local LGBTQ+ community for events and portraits. Photography is one of the most direct ways I can use my time and equipment to document and celebrate people.

Scouting, Honestly.

I'm an Eagle Scout (2011) and a Vigil Honor recipient in the Order of the Arrow (2012). I came up through Scouts during the exact period when the organization's policy toward gay youth and leaders was being publicly challenged — and I lived that transition firsthand, first in the closet and later as things started to open up. That experience is a big part of why community service, advocacy, and the quiet work of leaving things better than you found them matter so much to me now.

An Openly Gay Voice in Finance

Corporate finance hasn't historically been a space where openly gay men were especially visible. I'm proud to be one — not because it defines me, but because it's part of who I am, and I see no reason to leave it at the door. My work speaks for itself, and the people I work with know me as both a high-performing technologist and a gay man who's happy to broaden the conversation — sharing LGBTQ+ perspectives, history, and culture with colleagues who might not otherwise encounter them. That kind of everyday visibility, built on mutual respect and strong results, is how workplaces actually change.

Looking Forward

My long-term ambition sits at the intersection of policy, finance, and technology. I'm especially drawn to work that applies quantitative rigor to public-sector challenges — whether that's inside the Federal Reserve, the U.S. Treasury, the broader intelligence community, or (eventually) as an elected representative. The committees that speak most directly to my background are House Intelligence, Financial Services, and Commerce / Telecommunications.

Until then, I'm building the track record: ship rigorous work, write honestly about the economy and the world, and show up for the community I belong to.
