About

Finance, data science, and emerging technology — in one role.

I work at the intersection of financial modeling, data science, and applied AI inside Verizon's FP&A organization. I came up through the analyst ranks — Financial Analyst, then Senior Financial Analyst, now Corporate Financial Consultant — and across every one of those roles, my work has centered on building the models, forecasts, and executive reporting that Verizon's Consumer Group relies on: customer lifetime value, profitability, involuntary churn, bad debt, and device-margin KPIs.

What makes the work distinct is the toolkit. I pair traditional FP&A rigor with machine learning, productionalized GenAI pipelines (Claude and Gemini), and SQL-first analytics across Teradata and Oracle — automating the repetitive work that slows finance teams down and freeing up capacity for the decisions that actually matter.

My thesis on this work is simple: AI doesn't replace finance professionals — it becomes a force multiplier for them. I'm the go-to person on my team for automation, efficiency, and cross-functional data science collaboration, helping bring corporate finance into a new era where humans and AI work together to make faster, sharper decisions.

My approach is grounded in a deliberate academic foundation: a Juris Master in Financial Regulation & Compliance Law from the Florida State University College of Law, a Master of Science in Financial Technology from the University of Central Florida, and a Bachelor of Science in Statistics (with a minor in Computer Science) from UCF. I am currently pursuing a Master of Arts in Economics at the University of Florida, with a concentration in econometrics and data analysis.
